Buckingham Palace

Buckingham Palace has served as the official London residence of the UK’s sovereigns since 1837 and has 775 rooms which includes 19 State rooms, 52 Royal and guest bedrooms, 188 staff bedrooms, 92 offices and 78 bathrooms
Palace of Westminster (Houses of Parliament)

Westminster Palace, also known as the Houses of Parliament, is a Gothic style building where the Houses of Commons and Lords meet (the UK's two houses of Parliament)

National Gallery
An art museum in Trafalgar Square in the City of Westminster. Founded in 1824, in Trafalgar Square since 1838, it houses a collection of over 2,300 paintings dating from the mid-13th century to 1900
Marble Arch
A 19th-century white marble-faced triumphal arch designed by John Nash in 1827. In 1851 it was relocated to its current site. Following the widening of Park Lane in the early 1960s, the site became a large traffic island at the junction of three major roads, isolating the arch. Only the Royal Family and the King's Troop, Royal Horse Artillery are permitted to pass through the arch
Picadilly Circus
Famous for its neon signs, different displays and the Eros fountain in the middle, which is now one of London's top landmarks. Ther are many cinemas, theatres, shops and restaurants, including famous traditional English pubs
